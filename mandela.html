"... Living Legacies in the Sands of Time."

...The remarkable life of a great African Nationalist and founding father.

  • 1918 - 1943: Early life and Education from Mveto(a village in Umtata) to Johannesburg. He had this to say about his childhood:

    "No one in my family had ever attended school [...] On the first day of school my teacher, Miss Mdingane, gave each of us an English name. This was the custom among Africans in those days and was undoubtedly due to the British bias of our education.That day, Miss Mdingane told me that my new name was Nelson. Why this particular name I have no idea." — Mandela, 1994

  • 1943 - 1962: Revolutionary activities.
  • 1963 - 1990: Forced Imprisonment and Trials.
  • 1990 - 1994: End of Apartheid and General Elections.
  • 1994 - 1999: Presidency of South Africa
  • 1999 - 2013: Retirement, Continued Activism, Philanthropy, Illness and Death.

Mandiba

The life of Mandela is one filled with great humility, leadership and respect for all. During one of his trials and later imprisonment, he shared his views succintly in these words:

"I have fought against white domination, and I have fought against black domination. I have cherished the ideal of a democratic and free society in which all persons will live together in harmony and with equal opportunities. It is an ideal which I hope to live for and to see realised. But if it needs be, it is an ideal for which I am prepared to die." — Mandela's Rivonia Trial Speech, 1964
